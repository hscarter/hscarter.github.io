Haley Carter

I am a graduate student who uses genomics, controlled crosses, and field studies to understand the evolution of plant secondary chemistry.

I am fascinated by the way plants use chemistry in ecological interactions. Understanding the coevolutionary forces and the genomics behind phytochemical diversity drives passion for research and science. I am currently a student in the Plant Biology and Conservation Graduate Program at the Chicago Botanic Garden and Northwestern University. My graduate work combines biochemical and genetic analyses to understand how selection acts on the floral scent compound linalool in Oenothera harringtonii. Read more about my work here...
